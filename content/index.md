# CSSI — Certified Search & Seizure Instructor

Every case is verified against CourtListener and checked for good law. ## The Fourth Amendment — framework, analysis & justifications
- [[Fourth Amendment Framework]] — the spine: what the Amendment protects, the **two definitions of "search,"** seizure of property and of persons, the **state-action / private-search** doctrine, and the four-step analysis sequence.
- [[Fourth Amendment Analysis Checklist]] — the operational **checklist**: government action? → protected interest? → search or seizure? → standing? → reasonable (C.R.E.W.)?
- [[Two Definitions of Search]] — the **two independent theories** of a search: **trespass** (Olmstead → Silverman → *Jones*) and **privacy** (*Katz* → *Carpenter*); either suffices.
- [[Probable Cause and Reasonable Suspicion]] — the two **standards of proof**: PC (*Gates* totality, "fair probability") for arrests/searches/warrants and the lower **reasonable, articulable suspicion** for *Terry* stops; the hunch → RS → PC ladder; de novo review (*Ornelas*).
- [[Collective Knowledge and the Fellow-Officer Rule]] — imputing the PC/RS quantum across officers: **vertical** reliance on a directive/bulletin (*Whiteley*) and **horizontal** pooling; the limit — no PC at the source means no valid action (*Whiteley*; *Hensley* extends it to *Terry* stops).
- [[CREW]] — **C**onsent, **R**ecognized **E**xception, or **W**arrant — the three justifications at the reasonableness step.

## Protected areas
- [[Curtilage]] — the home's protected **curtilage** vs. the unprotected **open fields**; the *Dunn* four-factor test; *Jardines*' implied license.
- [[Abandonment]] — voluntary **abandonment** defeats standing (*Greenwood*); distinguish a **bailment** and **common authority** (*Bond*, *Matlock*).

## Seizures of persons & use of force
- [[Seizure of the Person]] — **when a person is seized**: physical force with intent to restrain (*Torres*) vs. submission to a show of authority (*Hodari D.*, *Mendenhall*); the **hunch → consensual encounter → reasonable suspicion** ladder.
- [[Terry Stops and Reasonable Suspicion]] — the investigative detention on **reasonable, articulable suspicion** and the protective **frisk**: the *Terry* standard, the anonymous-tip spectrum (*Adams*, *Alabama v. White*, *Florida v. J.L.*, *Navarette*), flight in a high-crime area (*Wardlow*), and totality / no divide-and-conquer (*Cortez*, *Arvizu*).
- [[Use of Force]] — **use of force** as a seizure: objective reasonableness, the three factors, and deadly force (*Garner*, *Scott v. Harris*).

## Recognized exceptions to the warrant requirement
*The "Recognized Exception" bucket of C.R.E.W., taught in detail. Each is a justification for acting **without** a warrant — the burden is on the government.*
- [[Arrest in the Home]] — crossing the threshold to arrest: an arrest warrant + reason to believe the suspect is home (*Payton*), a **search** warrant for a third party's home (*Steagald*), and the exigency routes (hot pursuit *Santana*; no police-created exigency *King*; misdemeanor pursuit not categorical *Lange*).
- [[Exigent Circumstances and Hot Pursuit]] — the emergency exception: **hot pursuit** (*Hayden*, *Santana*; limited by *Welsh*/*Lange*), **destruction of evidence** (*McNeely* totality; *Mitchell*), and the gate — the exigency must be genuine and **not police-created** (*King*).
- [[Community Caretaking and Emergency Aid]] — the **emergency-aid** entry on an *objective* basis (*Brigham City*, *Fisher*); caretaking born in the car (*Cady*); and *Caniglia*'s limit — no freestanding community-caretaking exception for the **home**.
- [[Consent Searches]] — the three prongs: **voluntariness** (totality, no warning required — *Schneckloth*), **authority** (actual/apparent; the objecting co-tenant *Randolph*), and **scope** (objective reasonableness — *Jimeno*).
- [[Knock and Talk]] — the consent-based front-door approach under the same **implied license** a visitor has (*Jardines*); knocking is lawful (*King*) but exceeding the license's scope/time/manner converts it into a search (*French v. Merrill*, 1st Cir.).
- [[Plain View Doctrine]] — seizing what is in plain view: *Horton*'s three prongs (lawful vantage · incriminating nature immediately apparent · lawful right of access); *Hicks* (no manipulation); the **digital-search** frontier.
- [[Automobile Exception]] — probable cause + a readily-mobile vehicle = a warrantless search of anywhere the object may be, containers included (*Carroll*, *Ross*, *Acevedo*); the curtilage limit (*Collins*).
- [[Search Incident to Arrest]] — the arrestee's person + wingspan (*Chimel*, *Robinson*), but **not** phone data (*Riley*); vehicle SITA (*Belton* cabined by *Gant*); and the administratively-distinct **booking/inventory** search (*Lafayette*, *Opperman*).
- [[Traffic Stops]] — pretext is irrelevant if there is an objective violation (*Whren*); a stop may last only as long as its **mission** (*Rodriguez*; a dog sniff is no search but may not add time, *Caballes*); passengers are seized too (*Brendlin*) and may be frisked on reasonable suspicion (*Arizona v. Johnson*).
- [[Special Needs and Administrative Searches]] — reasonableness-balancing searches beyond ordinary law enforcement: sobriety checkpoints (*Sitz*; the *Edmond* primary-purpose limit), schools (*T.L.O.*), probation/parole (*Griffin*, *Knights*, *Samson*), drug testing, and administrative inspections (*Camara*); inventory cross-links [[Search Incident to Arrest]].
- [[Border Searches]] — at the border the sovereign interest is at its zenith: **routine** searches need no suspicion; **non-routine** searches need reasonable suspicion (*Montoya de Hernandez*); the unresolved **device forensic-search split** (9th Cir. *Cotterman*/*Cano* vs. 11th Cir. *Touset*).
- [[Securing the Scene]] — controlling people and space around a search/arrest: **detain** occupants during warrant execution (*Summers*, *Muehler*; limited to the immediate vicinity by *Bailey*), the **protective sweep** (*Buie*'s two prongs), and the **freeze** of a home pending a warrant (*McArthur*).

## The warrant requirement
- [[The Warrant Requirement]] — what a valid warrant requires and how it fails: **probable cause** shown to a **neutral and detached magistrate** (*Johnson*; *Lo-Ji Sales*; *Coolidge*), **particularity** (*Groh*, *Garrison*, *Andresen*), the *Franks* challenge to a false affidavit, **knock-and-announce** (*Wilson*, *Richards*, *Hudson*), and **anticipatory** warrants (*Grubbs*).

## Remedies, standing & accountability
- [[The Exclusionary Rule]] — the **suppression remedy**: a judicially-created **deterrent** (not a personal right) barring unlawfully-obtained evidence and its **fruits** (*Wong Sun*; *Weeks*/*Mapp*) — and the exceptions that still let evidence in: **inevitable discovery** (*Nix*), **independent source** (*Murray*), **attenuation** (*Strieff*), and **good faith** (*Leon*).
- [[Standing to Challenge a Search]] — 4A rights are **personal**: only a defendant whose **own** reasonable expectation of privacy was invaded may suppress (*Rakas*); possession is not automatic standing (*Rawlings*, *Salvucci*); the overnight guest (*Olson*) vs. the transient visitor (*Carter*); the unauthorized rental driver (*Byrd*).
- [[Section 1983 Liability and Qualified Immunity]] — civil liability for rights deprivations under color of law (§ 1983; § 1988 fees; criminal § 242), **qualified immunity** and "clearly established" law (*Harlow*; *al-Kidd*, *Mullenix*; *Hope v. Pelzer*).
- [[Brady and Giglio]] — the prosecution's due-process duty to disclose **exculpatory** (*Brady*) and **impeachment** (*Giglio*) evidence — including material known only to the **police** (*Kyles*); the "Giglio-impaired" officer.

## Confessions & identifications (Fifth & Sixth Amendment — suppression of statements & IDs)
- [[Due-Process Voluntariness of Confessions]] — the pre-Miranda line that still governs: a confession produced by coercion that **overbears the will** is inadmissible, judged by the **totality** (*Brown*, *Ashcraft*, *Spano*); coercion must be **police** conduct (*Connelly*).
- [[Miranda and Custodial Interrogation]] — warnings are required before **custodial interrogation**: **custody** (objective — *Berkemer*, *Howes*, *J.D.B.*) and **interrogation** (express questioning or its functional equivalent — *Innis*); the public-safety exception (*Quarles*); *Miranda* is constitutional (*Dickerson*).
- [[Miranda Waiver and Invocation]] — after warnings: **waiver** (express or implied — *Butler*, *Thompkins*) vs. **invocation** — counsel must be unambiguous (*Davis*) and triggers the *Edwards* bar; silence must be scrupulously honored (*Mosley*); the break-in-custody rule (*Shatzer*) and statement-fruits (*Elstad*, *Patane*, *Seibert*).
- [[Sixth Amendment Right to Counsel]] — attaches at the **initiation of adversary judicial proceedings** (*Kirby*) and is **offense-specific** (*Cobb*); after attachment, **deliberate elicitation** without counsel is barred (*Massiah*, *Brewer v. Williams*, *Henry*); distinct from the 5A *Miranda* right.
- [[Eyewitness Identification]] — the 6A right to counsel at **post-charge corporeal lineups** (*Wade*/*Gilbert*; not pre-charge *Kirby*, not photos *Ash*) and the due-process bar on **unnecessarily suggestive** identifications judged by reliability (*Biggers*, *Manson*; *Perry*).

## Substantive defenses (adjacent)
- [[Entrapment]] — a **substantive defense** (not a suppression doctrine): the federal **subjective** test focuses on the defendant's **predisposition** (*Sorrells*, *Sherman*, *Jacobson*, *Russell*); the government may furnish opportunity but not implant criminal design in an unwilling person.
