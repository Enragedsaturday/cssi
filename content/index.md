# CSSI — Certified Search & Seizure Instructor

**Federal standard · criminal-suppression focus · Days 1–3.** Every doctrine page states the *federal* rule — U.S. Supreme Court binding; circuit courts persuasive (splits flagged); state cases appear only as clearly-labeled, non-binding illustrations paired with the controlling federal authority. Every case is verified against CourtListener.

## Foundations & history
- [[Common Law Origins]] — the English & colonial roots of the Amendment: the general warrant and the writ of assistance; Paxton's Case, *Wilkes v. Wood*, *Entick v. Carrington*, the state declarations of rights, and Madison — grounded in *Boyd* and *Riley*.

## Legal system, research & reference
- [[The Federal Court System]] — the three-tier hierarchy (district → **13 courts of appeals** → SCOTUS); **vertical vs. horizontal stare decisis**; binding vs. persuasive and the **circuit split**; SCOTUS composition, **certiorari** and the **rule of four**; judicial review (*Marbury*).
- [[Legal Research and Case Citations]] — how to **read a Bluebook citation** (volume · reporter · page · pinpoint · court & year), published vs. unpublished opinions, and how to **research case law for free** (CourtListener, Google Scholar, Justia) and verify it is still good law.
- [[Common Legal Terms]] — a working **glossary** of the Latin and procedural terms an instructor needs (*mens rea*, *habeas corpus*, *prima facie*, dicta vs. holding, *stare decisis*, *voir dire*, …) with plain-English examples.

## The Fourth Amendment — framework, analysis & justifications
- [[Fourth Amendment Framework]] — the spine: what the Amendment protects, the **two definitions of "search,"** seizure of property and of persons, the **state-action / private-search** doctrine, and the four-step analysis sequence.
- [[Fourth Amendment Analysis Checklist]] — the operational **checklist**: government action? → protected interest? → search or seizure? → standing? → reasonable (C.R.E.W.)?
- [[Two Definitions of Search]] — the **two independent theories** of a search: **trespass** (Olmstead → Silverman → *Jones*) and **privacy** (*Katz* → *Carpenter*); either suffices.
- [[CREW]] — **C**onsent, **R**ecognized **E**xception, or **W**arrant — the three justifications at the reasonableness step.

## Protected areas
- [[Curtilage]] — the home's protected **curtilage** vs. the unprotected **open fields**; the *Dunn* four-factor test; *Jardines*' implied license.
- [[Abandonment]] — voluntary **abandonment** defeats standing (*Greenwood*); distinguish a **bailment** and **common authority** (*Bond*, *Matlock*).

## Seizures of persons & use of force
- [[Seizure of the Person]] — **when a person is seized**: physical force with intent to restrain (*Torres*) vs. submission to a show of authority (*Hodari D.*, *Mendenhall*); the **hunch → consensual encounter → reasonable suspicion** ladder.
- [[Terry Stops and Reasonable Suspicion]] — the investigative detention on **reasonable, articulable suspicion** and the protective **frisk**: the *Terry* stop/frisk standard, the anonymous-tip spectrum (*Adams*, *Alabama v. White*, *Florida v. J.L.*, *Navarette*), flight in a high-crime area (*Wardlow*), and totality / no divide-and-conquer (*Cortez*, *Arvizu*).
- [[Use of Force]] — **use of force** as a seizure: objective reasonableness, the three factors, and deadly force (*Garner*, *Scott v. Harris*).

## Recognized exceptions to the warrant requirement
*The "Recognized Exception" bucket of C.R.E.W., taught in detail. Each is a justification for acting **without** a warrant — the burden is on the government.*
- [[Arrest in the Home]] — crossing the threshold to arrest: an arrest warrant + reason to believe the suspect is home (*Payton*), a **search** warrant for a third party's home (*Steagald*), and the exigency routes (hot pursuit *Santana*; no police-created exigency *King*; misdemeanor pursuit not categorical *Lange*).
- [[Community Caretaking and Emergency Aid]] — the **emergency-aid** entry on an *objective* basis (*Brigham City*, *Fisher*); caretaking born in the car (*Cady*); and *Caniglia*'s limit — no freestanding community-caretaking exception for the **home**.
- [[Consent Searches]] — the three prongs: **voluntariness** (totality, no warning required — *Schneckloth*; custody is only a factor — *Watson*), **authority** (actual/apparent; the objecting co-tenant *Randolph*), and **scope** (objective reasonableness — *Jimeno*).
- [[Plain View Doctrine]] — seizing what is in plain view: *Horton*'s three prongs (lawful vantage · incriminating nature immediately apparent · lawful right of access); *Hicks* (no manipulation); and the **digital-search** frontier (*Tuggle*, *Morton*).
- [[Automobile Exception]] — probable cause + a readily-mobile vehicle = a warrantless search of anywhere the object may be, containers included (*Carroll*, *Ross*, *Acevedo*); the curtilage limit (*Collins*).
- [[Search Incident to Arrest]] — the arrestee's person + wingspan (*Chimel*, *Robinson*), but **not** phone data (*Riley*); vehicle SITA (*Belton* cabined by *Gant*); and the administratively-distinct **booking/inventory** search (*Lafayette*, *Opperman*).
- [[Traffic Stops]] — pretext is irrelevant if there is an objective violation (*Whren*); a stop may last only as long as its **mission** (*Rodriguez*; a dog sniff is no search but may not add time, *Caballes*); passengers are seized too (*Brendlin*) and may be frisked on reasonable suspicion (*Arizona v. Johnson*); a protective vehicle frisk on reasonable suspicion of a weapon (*Long*).

## Remedies, accountability & disclosure
- [[The Exclusionary Rule]] — the **suppression remedy**: a judicially-created **deterrent** (not a personal right) barring unlawfully-obtained evidence and its **fruits** (*Wong Sun*; *Weeks*/*Mapp*) — and the exceptions that still let evidence in: **standing** (*Rakas*), **inevitable discovery** (*Nix*), **independent source** (*Murray*), **attenuation** (*Strieff*), and **good faith** (*Leon*).
- [[Section 1983 Liability and Qualified Immunity]] — civil liability for rights deprivations under color of law (§ 1983; § 1988 fees; criminal § 242), **qualified immunity** and "clearly established" law (*Harlow*; precedent must put the question "beyond debate," not framed at a high level of generality — *al-Kidd*, *Mullenix* — unless the violation is obvious, *Hope v. Pelzer*), and why there is no "good-faith" pass for a warrantless search — only objective reasonableness.
- [[Brady and Giglio]] — the prosecution's due-process duty to disclose **exculpatory** (*Brady*) and **impeachment** (*Giglio*) evidence — including material known only to the **police** (*Kyles*); the "Giglio-impaired" officer.

## Instructor maxims & practical habits
- [[Three Golden Rules]] — articulate *why*; the more serious the circumstance, the more reasonable the action is viewed; the Amendment deals in **probabilities, not possibilities** (includes **"Strive for Five"** and the burden-of-proof ladder).
- [[Instructor Development]] — the nine marks of an effective CSSI instructor: subject mastery, engagement, clear communication, "change the facts, change the outcome," and **making good case law**.
- [[CREW]] — the C.R.E.W. mnemonic (also a framework page, above).
