# SPEC — Miranda and Custodial Interrogation
slug: `miranda-custodial-interrogation` · bucket: `confessions-id` · status: NEW · template: Rule · Key cases (table: case · holding · weight · CL link) · Nuances & limits · Common pitfalls · Visual (Mermaid if multi-factor) · Flashcards (2-5 ::) · Sources

## Scope
Miranda warnings are required before CUSTODIAL INTERROGATION. The two triggers (the instructor's 'C's'): CUSTODY (objective — would a reasonable person feel free to leave / formal-arrest-equivalent restraint) and INTERROGATION (express questioning or its functional equivalent — Innis). Public-safety exception (Quarles). Miranda is constitutional (Dickerson).

## Required cases by ROLE (status: MANIFEST=manifest-grade · DECK=NTC existence+holding+url · P1=verified Phase 1 · GAP=verify Phase 3)
  - **Miranda v. Arizona** — _ANCHOR — warnings required to protect the 5A privilege during custodial interrogation_ · SCOTUS · `MANIFEST/DECK`
  - **Rhode Island v. Innis** — _ANCHOR — 'interrogation' = express questioning OR its functional equivalent_ · SCOTUS · `P1`
  - **Berkemer v. McCarty** — _PROGENY — ordinary traffic stop is not custody; misdemeanor/felony irrelevant_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Oregon v. Mathiason** — _PROGENY — voluntary station-house interview, not in custody_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Orozco v. Texas** — _PROGENY — custody can exist in the suspect's own bedroom (surrounded, not free to leave)_ · SCOTUS · `GAP`
  - **J.D.B. v. North Carolina** — _PROGENY — a child's age is part of the objective custody analysis_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **New York v. Quarles** — _LIMIT — public-safety exception allows un-Mirandized questions to neutralize immediate danger_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Dickerson v. United States** — _ANCHOR — Miranda is a constitutional rule Congress cannot override (18 U.S.C. 3501)_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`

## Visual (Mermaid)
Miranda gate: CUSTODY (objective: reasonable person not free to leave / arrest-equivalent) AND INTERROGATION (express OR functional equivalent — Innis) → warnings required. Exception: public safety (Quarles).

## Common pitfalls (must cover)
  - thinking Miranda applies to all police questioning (needs custody+interrogation)
  - treating a Terry/traffic stop as custody (Berkemer)
  - volunteered statements are not 'interrogation'
