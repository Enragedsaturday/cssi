# SPEC — Collective Knowledge and the Fellow-Officer Rule
slug: `collective-knowledge` · bucket: `seizures-persons` · status: NEW · template: Rule · Key cases (table: case · holding · weight · CL link) · Nuances & limits · Common pitfalls · Visual (Mermaid if multi-factor) · Flashcards (2-5 ::) · Sources

## Scope
When the knowledge of one officer is imputed to another. VERTICAL (an officer may act on a directive/bulletin from another who has PC/RS — but the issuing officer must actually have had it) and HORIZONTAL (facts known across cooperating officers are pooled). Federal rule; circuit applications of horizontal pooling flagged as persuasive.

## Required cases by ROLE (status: MANIFEST=manifest-grade · DECK=NTC existence+holding+url · P1=verified Phase 1 · GAP=verify Phase 3)
  - **Whiteley v. Warden** — _ANCHOR — may rely on a bulletin assuming PC, but if issuer lacked PC the arrest is invalid_ · SCOTUS · `P1`
  - **United States v. Hensley** — _ANCHOR — extends Whiteley to Terry stops on a wanted flyer issued on RS_ · SCOTUS · `P1`
