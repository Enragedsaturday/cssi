# SPEC — Standing to Challenge a Search
slug: `standing-to-challenge-a-search` · bucket: `remedies-standing` · status: NEW · template: Rule · Key cases (table: case · holding · weight · CL link) · Nuances & limits · Common pitfalls · Visual (Mermaid if multi-factor) · Flashcards (2-5 ::) · Sources

## Scope
Only a person whose OWN reasonable expectation of privacy (or possessory interest) was invaded may move to suppress — 4A rights are personal (Rakas). Possession does not automatically confer standing (Rawlings; Salvucci abolished automatic standing). Covers actual vs constructive possession, joint possession, and willful blindness as they bear on a privacy/possessory interest.

## Required cases by ROLE (status: MANIFEST=manifest-grade · DECK=NTC existence+holding+url · P1=verified Phase 1 · GAP=verify Phase 3)
  - **Rakas v. Illinois** — _ANCHOR — 4A rights are personal; reframes 'standing' as whether YOUR REP was violated; mere passengers w/o possessory interest cannot challenge a car search_ · SCOTUS · `MANIFEST`
  - **Jones v. United States** — _HISTORY — 'legitimately on premises' standing + automatic standing; both later rejected (Rakas/Salvucci)_ · SCOTUS · `GAP`
  - **United States v. Salvucci** — _PROGENY — abolishes automatic standing; possession of seized goods ≠ standing_ · SCOTUS · `GAP`
  - **Rawlings v. Kentucky** — _PROGENY — ownership of seized drugs in another's purse did not confer a REP in the purse_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Minnesota v. Olson** — _PROGENY — an overnight guest has a REP in the host's home_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Minnesota v. Carter** — _LIMIT — a short-term commercial visitor (bagging drugs) lacks a REP_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Byrd v. United States** — _PROGENY — an unauthorized driver of a rental car can still have a REP_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Brendlin v. California** — _PROGENY — a passenger is seized in a traffic stop and may challenge the stop_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`

## Common pitfalls (must cover)
  - equating ownership of the item with standing (Rawlings/Salvucci)
  - 'automatic standing' (gone)
  - confusing constructive possession (a guilt concept) with 4A standing — note willful blindness is a mens-rea doctrine, not a standing rule
