# SPEC — Knock and Talk
slug: `knock-and-talk` · bucket: `exceptions` · status: NEW · template: Rule · Key cases (table: case · holding · weight · CL link) · Nuances & limits · Common pitfalls · Visual (Mermaid if multi-factor) · Flashcards (2-5 ::) · Sources

## Scope
The 'knock and talk': officers may approach a home, knock, and seek a consensual conversation/consent to search under the same implied license a visitor has (Jardines). It is consent-based, not an exception unto itself; exceeding the implied license (scope, time, manner) converts it into a search. Shows the doctrine's current direction via circuit law.

## Required cases by ROLE (status: MANIFEST=manifest-grade · DECK=NTC existence+holding+url · P1=verified Phase 1 · GAP=verify Phase 3)
  - **Florida v. Jardines** — _ANCHOR — implied license to approach front door; a drug dog on the porch exceeds it (trespass theory)_ · SCOTUS · `MANIFEST/DECK`
  - **Kentucky v. King** — _ANCHOR — knocking & announcing presence is lawful; police may not create the exigency by threatening to violate the 4A_ · SCOTUS · `MANIFEST/DECK`
  - **French v. Merrill** — _SPLIT/PROGENY — 1st Cir.: officers exceeded the implied license; knock-and-talk has scope/time/manner limits (persuasive; 'which way the wind blows')_ · Circuit (1st) · `P1`

## Circuit splits / federal-state to flag
  - lower courts vary on how far a knock-and-talk may go (repeated knocks, back doors, pre-dawn) — French v. Merrill is the persuasive 1st-Cir. marker; label as circuit, not nationwide

## Visual (Mermaid)
Approach front door (implied license) → knock, seek consent → within license = OK; exceed scope/time/manner (back door, dog, refuse-to-leave) = search needing a warrant/exception.

## Common pitfalls (must cover)
  - treating knock-and-talk as its own warrant exception
  - ignoring curtilage/implied-license limits
  - using it to manufacture exigency (King)
