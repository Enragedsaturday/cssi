# SPEC — Border Searches
slug: `border-searches` · bucket: `exceptions` · status: NEW · template: Rule · Key cases (table: case · holding · weight · CL link) · Nuances & limits · Common pitfalls · Visual (Mermaid if multi-factor) · Flashcards (2-5 ::) · Sources

## Scope
At the international border (and its functional equivalent) the sovereign's interest is at its zenith: ROUTINE searches need no warrant, PC, or suspicion. NON-ROUTINE/highly intrusive searches (prolonged detention, body cavity) need reasonable suspicion. The unsettled frontier: forensic searches of electronic devices — an explicit circuit split.

  - **United States v. Montoya de Hernandez** — _ANCHOR — non-routine (alimentary-canal) detention needs reasonable suspicion_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **United States v. Flores-Montano** — _PROGENY — routine vehicle fuel-tank disassembly needs no suspicion_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **United States v. Martinez-Fuerte** — _PROGENY — fixed interior immigration checkpoints OK suspicionless_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Almeida-Sanchez v. United States** — _LIMIT — roving patrol far from border needs PC/warrant_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **United States v. Cotterman** — _SPLIT — 9th Cir.: forensic device search at border needs reasonable suspicion_ · Circuit (9th) · `GAP`
  - **United States v. Touset** — _SPLIT — 11th Cir.: no suspicion required even for forensic device search_ · Circuit (11th) · `DECK`
