# SPEC — Probable Cause and Reasonable Suspicion
slug: `probable-cause-reasonable-suspicion` · bucket: `4a-framework` · status: NEW · template: Rule · Key cases (table: case · holding · weight · CL link) · Nuances & limits · Common pitfalls · Visual (Mermaid if multi-factor) · Flashcards (2-5 ::) · Sources

## Scope
The two key standards of proof in the Fourth Amendment: probable cause (fair probability) for arrests/searches/warrants, and the lower reasonable-articulable-suspicion for Terry stops. Totality of the circumstances; quantum, sources, and review. The page the Framework/Checklist point to for 'how much proof.'

## Required cases by ROLE (status: MANIFEST=manifest-grade · DECK=NTC existence+holding+url · P1=verified Phase 1 · GAP=verify Phase 3)
  - **Illinois v. Gates** — _ANCHOR — totality test for PC; abandons rigid Aguilar-Spinelli two-prong_ · SCOTUS · `MANIFEST/DECK`
  - **Aguilar v. Texas / Spinelli v. United States** — _HISTORY — the two-prong test Gates replaced_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Maryland v. Pringle** — _PROGENY — PC is particularized but can extend to all occupants_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Brinegar v. United States** — _ANCHOR — classic PC definition ('fair probability'/practical probabilities)_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Terry v. Ohio** — _ANCHOR — reasonable suspicion < PC for a brief stop/frisk_ · SCOTUS · `MANIFEST`
  - **Ornelas v. United States** — _PROGENY — de novo appellate review of RS/PC determinations_ · SCOTUS · `GAP`
  - **Devenpeck v. Alford** — _PROGENY — PC judged objectively; offense need not be closely related to stated charge_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **District of Columbia v. Wesby** — _PROGENY — totality; courts must not divide-and-conquer facts_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Florida v. Harris** — _PROGENY — dog-sniff alert can supply PC; totality not rigid checklist_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Illinois v. Wardlow** — _ILLUS/PROGENY — RS from headlong flight in high-crime area (cross-link Terry)_ · SCOTUS · `MANIFEST`
