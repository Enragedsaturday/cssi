# SPEC — Securing the Scene: Detention, Protective Sweeps and Freezes
slug: `securing-the-scene` · bucket: `exceptions` · status: NEW · template: Rule · Key cases (table: case · holding · weight · CL link) · Nuances & limits · Common pitfalls · Visual (Mermaid if multi-factor) · Flashcards (2-5 ::) · Sources

## Scope
What officers may do to secure premises and people around a search/arrest: DETAIN occupants during warrant execution (Summers/Muehler, spatially limited by Bailey), conduct a PROTECTIVE SWEEP (Buie's two prongs), FREEZE a residence while a warrant is obtained (McArthur), and the limits on warrantless entry/SACO (constructive entry — Conner). Authority to enter-and-exclude flows from Payton.

## Required cases by ROLE (status: MANIFEST=manifest-grade · DECK=NTC existence+holding+url · P1=verified Phase 1 · GAP=verify Phase 3)
  - **Michigan v. Summers** — _ANCHOR — warrant to search premises carries limited authority to detain occupants during the search_ · SCOTUS · `P1`
  - **Muehler v. Mena** — _PROGENY — may handcuff/detain occupants for the search's duration where reasonable; collateral questioning not a separate seizure_ · SCOTUS · `P1`
  - **Bailey v. United States** — _LIMIT — Summers detention limited to the immediate vicinity; cannot detain a former occupant a mile away_ · SCOTUS · `P1`
  - **Maryland v. Buie** — _ANCHOR — protective sweep: adjoining spaces w/o suspicion; broader sweep on reasonable, articulable suspicion_ · SCOTUS · `P1`
  - **Illinois v. McArthur** — _ANCHOR — may temporarily prevent entry to a home (a reasonable 'freeze') while obtaining a warrant_ · SCOTUS · `P1`
  - **Payton v. New York** — _ANCHOR — arrest warrant + reason to believe suspect home permits entry & authority to exclude/control_ · SCOTUS · `MANIFEST`
  - **United States v. Conner** — _SPLIT/CONSTRUCTIVE-ENTRY — 8th Cir.: opening a door under color of authority = constructive entry/search (NOT framed as seizure-of-person; see CONNER-FRAMING)_ · Circuit (8th) · `P1`

## Visual (Mermaid)
During warrant execution: Detain occupants (Summers) — limited to immediate vicinity (Bailey); Protective sweep (Buie: adjoining=no suspicion / beyond=RS); Freeze premises pending warrant (McArthur).

## Common pitfalls (must cover)
  - detaining people who already left the scene (Bailey)
  - treating a protective sweep as a full search
  - SACO framed as a mere consensual encounter (it's constructive entry)
