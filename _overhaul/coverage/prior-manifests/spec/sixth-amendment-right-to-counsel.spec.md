# SPEC — Sixth Amendment Right to Counsel
slug: `sixth-amendment-right-to-counsel` · bucket: `confessions-id` · status: NEW · template: Rule · Key cases (table: case · holding · weight · CL link) · Nuances & limits · Common pitfalls · Visual (Mermaid if multi-factor) · Flashcards (2-5 ::) · Sources

## Scope
The 6A right to counsel attaches at the initiation of adversary judicial proceedings (Kirby) and is OFFENSE-SPECIFIC (Cobb). Once attached, deliberate elicitation of statements without counsel violates it (Massiah; Brewer v. Williams 'Christian burial speech'; Henry/jailhouse informants). Distinct from the 5A Miranda-counsel right.

## Visual (Mermaid)

## Common pitfalls (must cover)
  - confusing 5A Miranda-counsel with 6A counsel (different triggers/scope)
  - forgetting offense-specificity (Cobb)
  - thinking attachment occurs at arrest (it's at charging — Kirby)
