# SPEC — Miranda: Waiver and Invocation
slug: `miranda-waiver-invocation` · bucket: `confessions-id` · status: NEW · template: Rule · Key cases (table: case · holding · weight · CL link) · Nuances & limits · Common pitfalls · Visual (Mermaid if multi-factor) · Flashcards (2-5 ::) · Sources

## Scope
After warnings: a suspect may WAIVE (knowing, intelligent, voluntary — and may be implied by conduct, Thompkins) or INVOKE. Invocation of counsel must be unambiguous (Davis) and triggers Edwards' bright-line bar on re-initiation; invocation of silence must be scrupulously honored (Mosley). Break-in-custody (Shatzer). Fruits of un-warned but voluntary statements (Elstad/Patane) vs deliberate two-step (Seibert).

## Required cases by ROLE (status: MANIFEST=manifest-grade · DECK=NTC existence+holding+url · P1=verified Phase 1 · GAP=verify Phase 3)
  - **North Carolina v. Butler** — _ANCHOR — waiver need not be express; can be inferred from conduct_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Berghuis v. Thompkins** — _PROGENY — silence is not invocation; waiver implied by answering after warnings_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Edwards v. Arizona** — _ANCHOR — once counsel is invoked, police may not re-initiate until counsel present_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Michigan v. Mosley** — _PROGENY — invocation of silence must be 'scrupulously honored' (re-questioning on a different crime OK)_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Davis v. United States** — _LIMIT — invocation of counsel must be unambiguous ('maybe I should talk to a lawyer' insufficient)_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Maryland v. Shatzer** — _LIMIT — a 14-day break in custody ends Edwards protection_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Oregon v. Elstad** — _PROGENY — a later warned, voluntary confession is admissible despite an earlier un-warned one_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Missouri v. Seibert** — _LIMIT — deliberate 'question-first, warn-later' two-step is invalid_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **United States v. Patane** — _PROGENY — physical fruits of an un-warned but voluntary statement are admissible_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`

## Visual (Mermaid)
After warnings: Waive (express or implied-Thompkins) → questioning OK; Invoke counsel (unambiguous-Davis) → Edwards bar until counsel/14-day break (Shatzer); Invoke silence → scrupulously honor (Mosley).

## Common pitfalls (must cover)
  - thinking silence alone invokes (Thompkins)
  - treating an ambiguous reference to a lawyer as invocation (Davis)
  - ignoring the difference between invoking silence vs counsel
