# SPEC — Exigent Circumstances and Hot Pursuit
slug: `exigent-circumstances` · bucket: `exceptions` · status: NEW · template: Rule · Key cases · Nuances & limits · Common pitfalls · Visual · Flashcards · Sources

## Scope
The exigency exception: police may act without a warrant when the needs of the moment make obtaining one impracticable — HOT PURSUIT of a fleeing suspect, imminent destruction of evidence, and risk to life/safety (emergency aid is treated on [[Community Caretaking and Emergency Aid]]). The exigency must be genuine and not police-created; the gravity of the offense matters. This is the hub the other entry doctrines point into.

## BOUNDARY
Authority to ENTER on an emergency lives here; authority to enter to ARREST on a warrant → [[Arrest in the Home]] (Payton/Steagald); what officers do once lawfully inside → [[Securing the Scene: Detention, Protective Sweeps and Freezes]]; objective emergency-aid entries → [[Community Caretaking and Emergency Aid]].

## Required cases by ROLE (all corpus-verified)
  - **Warden v. Hayden** — _ANCHOR — hot-pursuit warrantless entry & search for a fleeing armed robber_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **United States v. Santana** — _PROGENY — hot pursuit begun in a public place may continue into the home_ · SCOTUS · `DECK/MANIFEST(arrest-in-home)`
  - **Welsh v. Wisconsin** — _LIMIT — the minor/nonjailable nature of the offense weighs heavily against warrantless home entry_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Lange v. California** — _LIMIT — pursuit of a fleeing MISDEMEANANT does not categorically justify warrantless home entry; case-by-case (GOOD-LAW WATCH per D-5)_ · SCOTUS · `DECK/MANIFEST(arrest-in-home)`
  - **Kentucky v. King** — _LIMIT — police may not rely on an exigency they created by threatening to violate the 4A_ · SCOTUS · `MANIFEST/DECK`
  - **Missouri v. McNeely** — _LIMIT — natural dissipation of BAC is not a per se exigency; totality_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Mitchell v. Wisconsin** — _PROGENY — unconscious-driver blood draw usually permissible under exigency_ · SCOTUS · `DECK`
  - **Brigham City v. Stuart** — _cross-link — objective emergency-aid entry (primary home: Community Caretaking)_ · SCOTUS · `MANIFEST/DECK`
